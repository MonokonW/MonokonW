Customer Portal Modernisation Programme
Version: 2.1
Status: Approved
Owner: Executive (J. Hargreaves)
Last reviewed: End of Stage 1
Next review: End of Stage 2
§ 1 Executive Summary Mandatory
The organisation's customer portal has not been materially updated since 2018. Customer satisfaction scores have declined from 78% to 61% over three years, and support call volumes have risen by 34%. This project will replace the existing portal with a modern, self-service platform expected to reduce support costs by £420,000 per annum and restore satisfaction scores above 80% within 12 months of go-live.

The Executive confirms the Business Case remains desirable, viable, and achievable as of the Stage 1 review. Total approved investment is £1.85M over 18 months.
§ 2 Reasons & Strategic Alignment Mandatory
The current portal cannot support mobile devices, requires manual staff intervention for 43% of customer requests, and operates on a vendor platform reaching end-of-life in Q3 next year. Failure to act will result in forced emergency migration, estimated at 2.4× the cost of planned modernisation, with no opportunity to capture the associated service improvement benefits.

This project directly supports the corporate strategic objective "Digital First by 2026" and is listed as a Priority 1 initiative in the current programme plan.

The Business Case as a Living Document
How it evolves through the PRINCE2 lifecycle
Pre-Project Outline Business Case Starting Up
The Executive creates an Outline Business Case as part of Starting Up a Project. It is brief — enough to justify whether initiating the project is worthwhile. It answers: what is the problem, what is a rough cost, and is there a plausible benefit? It does not need financial precision at this stage.
Initiation Full Business Case Developed Initiating
During Initiating a Project, the Business Case is fully developed. All nine sections are completed. Costs, timescales, investment appraisal, benefits with baselines, and the Benefits Management Approach are all agreed. The Project Board authorises the project based on this document. It becomes the baseline.
Each Stage Boundary Business Case Reviewed & Updated Mandatory
At every End Stage Review the Project Manager updates the Business Case with actual costs to date, revised forecasts, and any changes to benefits. The Project Board uses it to decide: continue, adjust, or stop? A Business Case that is never updated is a governance failure — the Board is being asked to make decisions blindly.

Exception Business Case Re-assessed If tolerance breached
If an Exception Report is raised — meaning a tolerance is forecast to be exceeded — the Project Board must re-examine the Business Case before authorising an Exception Plan. The project may still be viable, but the numbers must be re-tested. If the Business Case can no longer be justified, the project should be stopped.
Closure Final Update & Handover Mandatory
At project closure the Business Case is updated with final actuals and included in the End Project Report. The Benefits Management Approach — and accountability for post-project benefits reviews — is formally transferred to the Senior User. The project is over; benefits realisation has just begun.

Post-Project Benefits Realisation Reviews Senior User led
Benefits reviews are conducted at intervals agreed in the Benefits Management Approach — typically 6 and 12 months post go-live. The Senior User reports to Corporate or Programme management. These reviews are the only way to know whether the Business Case was ever actually true. Most organisations never conduct them.
